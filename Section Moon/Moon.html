Weight

The moon's mass is 7.35 x 1022 kg.

About 1.2% of Earth's mass. Put another way, Earth weighs 81 times more than the moon.

The moon's density is 3.34 grams per cubic centimeter (3.34 g/cm3).

Age

4.53 billion years

The Moon is Earth's only natural satellite. At about one-quarter the diameter of Earth, it is the largest natural satellite in the

Solar System relative to the size of a major planet, the fifth largest satellite in the Solar System overall, and larger than any known dwarf planet.

Distance

On average, the Earth and Moon are about 150 million kilometres (or 93 million miles) from the Sun!

That distance is so large that it takes light eight minutes to reach us

(remember that light travels at 300,000 kilometres per second).
